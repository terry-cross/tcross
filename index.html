Terry Cross Biography

Introduction to Careers

Looking back on my experience in the work force since graduating
from college I see where I have had 2 careers, and I am currently
pursuing a third. All three have involved developing technologies
and have conditioned me to keep a steady eye on what lies ahead
in the future.

    Career Paths
  • Television
  • Teaching
  • Technology

    • Video Production Technician

    When I began working in television broadcasting
    Sony released the chip camera which soon replaced tube cameras.
    Changes in the industry came regularly after that with changes
    in tape formats and recording equipment, character generators,
    live remotes and satellite transmissions,
    everything was being moved to a digital platform.
    I have developed a basic rule for adapting to changes:
    Learn how things are organized, and learn where to look.
    This rule has served me working as a Master Control Operator,
    as a hospital AV technician, and as TV producer director.

    CTE Instructor

    What I learned from working in television,
    I was able to relate to high school students because my background
    afforded me the role of being a "Jack of All Trades."
    I could teach students about shooting video and recording audio well,
    and how to conduct interviews in front of the camera.
    Behind the scene skills of writing and directing
    and producing allowed me to offer
    a broad array of training to large classes.
    The technical advances in the TV film industry
    were occuring rapidly and made it challenging
    for high school programs to offer training
    on up to date equipment. While they may not
    have received training on the latest industry tech,
    I was able to offer my students training in film anaylysis.
    As a career technical education instructor
    in the Arts Media Entertainment sector.
    I stressed professionalism to students, reminding them,
    "The only difference between an amatuer and a professional
    is that a professional knows how to trouble shoot."

    Software Engineer

    Working with computers became a more integral part
    of working in the field of video productions.
    Becoming familiar with computer applications,
    like Adobe Photoshop, became a necessity for operating
    character graphics in video productions.
    I made a transition from teaching video productions
    to teaching graphic design as a CTE Instructor
    after attending a Digital Learning Academy.
    At the Academy I studied Adobe Photoshop,
    Illustrator, and InDesign    , along with some
    HTML and Python, and 3D Printing.
    Getting a better handle on coding would
    definitely help me develop a stronger curriculum
    for my graphic design students. However, my son,
    who graduated from Loyola Marymount,
    was able to land his first job as a software engineer
    earning 6 figures.This has brought me
    to a new level of thinking in career choices.
    The evolution of technology has always reminded me
    to keep learning in order to remain relevant.
    With online learning becoming the new norm
    and my teaching career on hold with the Corona Virus,
    software engineering at Kenzie Academy
    may be the avenue to travel into a thrid career.
